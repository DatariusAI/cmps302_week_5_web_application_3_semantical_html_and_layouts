Tourism

The tourism industry began in the early 19th century when foreigners visited the Alps, travelled to the bases of the mountains to enjoy the scenery, and stayed at the spa-resorts. Large hotels were built during the Belle Epoque; cog-railways, built early in the 20th century, brought tourists to ever higher elevations, with the Jungfraubahn terminating at the Jungfraujoch, well above the eternal snow-line, after going through a tunnel in Eiger.
The Alps are one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world with many resorts such as:

  1. Oberstdorf, in Bavaria
    • Municipality and skiing and hiking town in southwest Germany
    • One of the highest market towns in Germany
  2. Saalbach in Austria
    • Municipality in the district of Zell am See
    • Well known for its skiing and other winter sports.
  3. Davos in Switzerland
    • Municipality in the district of Prättigau/Davos
    • Host to the World Economic Forum (WEF)
    • Home of one of Switzerland's biggest ski resorts
  4. Chamonix in France
    • Commune in the Haute-Savoie département
    • Site of the first Winter Olympics in 1924

Winter Sports

In the early 19th and 20th century, winter sports were slowly introduced...

  1. The 1924 Winter Olympics in Chamonix, France;
  2. The 1928 Winter Olympics in St. Moritz, Switzerland;
  3. The 1936 Winter Olympics in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany.

During World War II the winter games were cancelled but after that time the Winter Games have been held in St. Moritz (1948), Cortina d'Ampezzo (1956), Innsbruck (1964, 1976), Grenoble (1968), Albertville (1992), and Torino (2006).
